About The Starrys

A De Pere family team, twelve years in.

Starry Realty LLC opened its doors in 2014 on North Broadway in De Pere. The firm started the way most family businesses start: one person with a long track record stepping out to build something under their own name. Rich Starry had spent four decades in sales, marketing, and customer service before he ever wrote his first listing. His mother, Becki Starry, joined the practice. Mary Jo took the office. Twelve years later the three of them still run the firm out of the same building, two blocks from the Fox River.

A mother-son real estate team is unusual. A mother-son team that has held the same downtown De Pere address for twelve years is, as far as public search records suggest, the only one in Northeast Wisconsin.

The firm serves the De Pere and Green Bay relocation market: families moving into the area, longtime residents downsizing, first-time buyers learning the difference between a Wrightstown commute and a Howard one. Rich's four-decade sales background and Becki's mother's-eye for what families actually need at the kitchen table show up in how the firm runs. So does the choice to stay small enough that the same three people answer the phone every time you call.

What follows is a review of how that twelve-year story currently surfaces in the AI search layer that buyers increasingly consult before they ever pick up a phone.

What We'd Build

The structured-data work that makes a twelve-year family team legible to AI engines.

The work below is the standard schema and content build we deploy on family-team and small-brokerage prospects with strong heritage and weak machine-readable surface area. Every item below is publishable from facts the firm already controls.

01
Person schema for all three team members
Distinct Person markup for Rich Starry, Becki Starry, and Mary Jo. Each with role, organizational affiliation, and the address relationship that ties them to 112 N Broadway. Currently zero of three are individually queryable.
02
Family-team Organization page
A first-class "Our Team" page wired with Organization schema and worker relationships. The page becomes the source-of-truth surface AI engines cite when asked "who runs Starry Realty."
03
De Pere LocalBusiness schema
Full LocalBusiness markup for 112 N Broadway: founding date, service area for both De Pere and Green Bay, opening hours, and contact channels. Resolves the street-address query failure cleanly.
04
Relocation-services landing pages
Dedicated pages for relocation into De Pere and Green Bay, each wired with Service schema and FAQ schema covering the questions buyers actually ask before they move. Captures the "relocation expert" query category that currently goes to national portals.
05
Family-team origin-story page
A long-form origin page narrating Rich's 40-plus-year sales background, the 2014 founding, and Becki's joining the practice. The narrative surface that makes the mother-son category legible to an AI summarizer rather than just to a human reader.
06
FAQ schema for the five audited queries
Structured FAQ blocks answering each of the five queries above in the firm's own voice, on the firm's own domain. The fastest documented path from zero citations to first-page AI surface for category-defining queries.
Why Now

The window on uncontested category claims is closing as AI search matures.

Three forces are converging on small family brokerages this year. None of them are dramatic alone. Together they make the next twelve months meaningfully different from the last twelve.

1
Buyers are starting in AI
A growing share of relocation queries, "best realtor" searches, and neighborhood questions are being asked of AI engines before any traditional search engine is opened. Firms that are absent from the AI layer simply do not appear in those buyers' shortlists.

Common Questions

Questions Starry Realty might reasonably ask before any of this gets built.

The four answers below are the ones we field most often from heritage family firms in the same situation: long track record, real story, weak AI surface.

Is this an SEO project or an AI search project? They feel similar but the audits read differently.
They overlap but they are not the same. Classic SEO ranks pages in Google's blue-link results. AI search optimization, sometimes called AEO, decides whether a generative engine cites your firm by name when a buyer asks a question in natural language. The structured-data work below strengthens both, but the audit specifically measured the AI layer because that is the surface most under-served at twelve-year family brokerages.
Why does Starry Realty's actual website not already cover this?
The current Starry Realty site communicates the firm's story well to a human visitor. What it does not yet do is publish that story in the structured-data formats (Person, Organization, LocalBusiness, FAQ) that AI engines parse when assembling an answer. The narrative is intact. The machine-readable layer is missing. Those are two different builds.
Does this involve changing how Rich, Becki, or Mary Jo work day to day?
No. The build is entirely on the firm's web surface and structured-data layer. The day-to-day work of meeting buyers at houses, holding listings, and running closings is untouched. The goal is to make the work the firm already does findable in the channels buyers now use first.
What would the engagement actually look like in week one?
Week one is a kickoff conversation to confirm the facts above, gather real photography of the team and the office, and confirm the firm's preferred narrative voice. From there the schema build, the team page, and the relocation pages are scoped against the firm's existing website platform. Nothing in this audit assumes any particular CMS or hosting arrangement.
